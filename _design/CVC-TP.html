Lesson 1
What is an equation?
⚖️
A statement of equality
Two expressions linked by =. Both sides must balance — like a weighing scale.
🔢
Contains a variable
We use a letter (usually x) to represent an unknown value we want to find.
🎯
Our goal: isolate the variable
Use inverse operations on both sides to get x on its own.
2x + 3 = 11 ← find x
2 / 6
Solving linear equations
✅ Do this
1️⃣Apply the same operation to both sides
2️⃣Use inverse operations to undo terms
3️⃣Check your answer by substituting back
Example
2x + 6 = 14
2x = 8
x = 4 ✓
❌ Common mistakes
⚠️Only operating on one side of the equation
⚠️Sign errors when moving terms across =
⚠️Forgetting to distribute brackets before solving
Watch out
2x + 6 = 14
2x = 20 ✗
(added instead of subtracted)
3 / 6
Worked example
Solving step by step
1
Write the equation
3x − 9 = 3(x − 3)
2
Expand the bracket on the right
3x − 9 = 3x − 9
Distribute 3 × (x − 3) = 3x − 9
3
Subtract 3x from both sides
−9 = −9
The x terms cancel out completely
✓
Conclusion: infinitely many solutions
−9 = −9 is always true, so any value of x works. This is an identity.
4 / 6
Three types of solutions
One solution
2x + 1 = 5
x = 2
x cancels to a single value. The lines intersect at one point.
No solution
2x + 3 = 2x
3 = 0 ✗
Contradiction — no value of x makes this true. Parallel lines.
Infinite solutions
3x−9=3(x−3)
−9 = −9 ✓
Identity — always true for any x. The lines are identical.
